#### 2. The Technical Foundation: How AI "Sees" Depth
We are currently witnessing a shift away from "flat-masking" toward  **Diffusion-based Video Synthesis** . StoneSightAI does not merely overlay a texture; it utilizes  **Monocular Depth Estimation**  to infer the three-dimensional structure of a room from a single smartphone photo.
The AI reconstructs the environment through a rigorous three-step technical process:
1.  **Seed Interpretation:**  The system treats the 2D upload as a "latent seed," identifying primary perspective lines.
2.  **Structural Mapping:**  The engine generates a 3D "point cloud" or "Gaussian splat," creating a digital mannequin of the cabinetry, walls, and flooring.
3.  **Spatial Projection:**  The AI calculates the  **Epipolar Geometry**  between the original photo and the new virtual camera positions, ensuring perspective accuracy as the view rotates.

#### 3. Painting the Hidden Corners: Inpainting and Outpainting
When synthesizing a  **Dual 180° Video**  (generating both clockwise and anti-clockwise rotations), the AI inevitably encounters occluded angles—parts of the kitchen island or backsplash not captured in the original photo. To achieve a  **Complete 360° Vision** , the system utilizes  **Inpainting**  and  **Outpainting** .
Through  **Latent Space Manipulation** , the AI "hallucinates" these missing segments. Unlike random generation, the AI navigates a latent space of millions of architectural configurations to ensure the generated geometry is physically plausible. Successful synthesis requires:
*   **Architectural Consistency:**  Maintaining the structural integrity of walls and floor planes during camera movement.
*   **Material Fidelity:**  Ensuring the complex mineral veining of the stone remains stable and non-flickering throughout the rotation.
*   **Environmental Awareness:**  Detecting the specific light sources in the user's kitchen to ensure the generated sections feel "anchored" in the original environment.
#### 4. The Secret of Realism: Temporal Parallax and Light Interaction
The brain perceives a video as "real" primarily through  **Temporal Parallax** —the velocity difference between the foreground stone and background walls as the camera moves. This motion provides the "Temporal Proof" required to validate a material's presence.
However, the true differentiator of Generative AI is its ability to simulate  **Subsurface Scattering**  and  **Anisotropy** . Traditional renders look like printed plastic because they use "diffuse maps." StoneSightAI understands that light enters a translucent material like marble, scatters, and exits, creating an "inner glow." As the camera rotates, the AI calculates the  **reflectance shift**  within the mineral lattice:
$$R_{f} = R_{i} \times \cos(\theta)$$
In this formula, the AI navigates the latent space to predict how the reflection ( $R_f$ ) changes as the virtual camera angle ( $\theta$ ) shifts relative to the incident light ( $R_i$ ).
"Motion acts as the ultimate  **Trust Signal.**  By seeing specular highlights (reflections) move naturally across a stone's surface, the viewer's brain receives the physiological confirmation that the material has real depth, sheen, and value."
