### How to Disable Startup Items in Windows

1. **Open Task Manager**:
   - Press `Ctrl + Shift + Esc` on your keyboard. This opens Task Manager directly.
   - Alternatively, right-click the taskbar (bottom of the screen) and select "Task Manager".

2. **Navigate to the Startup Tab**:
   - In Task Manager, look at the tabs at the top (e.g., Processes, Performance, Startup).
   - Click the **Startup** tab. (If you don’t see tabs, click "More details" at the bottom first.)

3. **Review the List**:
   - You’ll see a list of programs that launch when your laptop starts.
   - Columns include:
     - **Name**: The program’s name (e.g., "Skype", "Realtek Audio").
     - **Publisher**: Who made it.
     - **Status**: Enabled or Disabled.
     - **Startup Impact**: How much it slows your boot (Low, Medium, High).

4. **Identify Items to Disable**:
   - Focus on programs with "High" or "Medium" startup impact that you don’t need immediately at boot.
   - Examples to consider disabling:
     - Chat apps (e.g., Discord, Skype) – you can open them manually later.
     - Cloud services (e.g., OneDrive, Dropbox) – if you don’t sync files constantly.
     - Software updaters (e.g., Adobe, Java) – they can check updates later.
     - Bloatware (e.g., pre-installed games or tools).
   - **Keep Enabled**: Antivirus software, essential drivers (e.g., graphics, audio), or anything critical to your workflow.

5. **Disable a Startup Item**:
   - Click the program you want to stop from starting automatically.
   - Click the **Disable** button at the bottom-right corner. (Or right-click the item and select "Disable".)
   - The "Status" will change from "Enabled" to "Disabled".

6. **Repeat as Needed**:
   - Go through the list and disable other unnecessary items. You can always re-enable them later if needed.

7. **Restart Your Laptop**:
   - Close Task Manager.
   - Restart your laptop (`Start > Power > Restart`) to apply changes.
   - Notice if it boots faster or feels less sluggish.

### Additional Tips
- **Not Sure What Something Is?**
  - Right-click an item and select "Search online" to learn more about it. Avoid disabling anything unfamiliar unless you confirm it’s safe.
- **Re-Enable If Needed**:
  - Return to the Startup tab, select a disabled item, and click "Enable".
- **Alternative Method (Advanced)**:
  - Use `Win + R`, type `msconfig`, and go to the "Startup" tab. This links to Task Manager in newer Windows versions but offers more system options if needed. Be cautious here.
